Search for a Disease

Malaria

Mode of Transmission: Spread through the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito.

Prevention: Use of insecticide-treated mosquito nets, indoor spraying, and antimalarial medications.

Treatment: Antimalarial drugs like artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs).

Cholera

Mode of Transmission: Spread through contaminated water or food.

Prevention: Ensure clean drinking water, proper sanitation, and food hygiene.

Treatment: Oral rehydration solutions (ORS), IV fluids, and antibiotics in severe cases.

Hepatitis B

Mode of Transmission: Spread through contact with infected blood or bodily fluids.

Prevention: Vaccination, use of sterile needles, and safe sex practices.

Treatment: Antiviral medications and monitoring for liver damage.

Typhoid Fever

Mode of Transmission: Spread through contaminated food and water.

Prevention: Vaccination, drinking clean water, and practicing good hygiene.

Treatment: Antibiotics such as ciprofloxacin and azithromycin.

Schistosomiasis (Bilharzia)

Mode of Transmission: Contact with contaminated freshwater where parasitic worms live.

Prevention: Avoid swimming in freshwater, improve sanitation, and snail control.

Treatment: Praziquantel is the most effective treatment.

Measles

Mode of Transmission: Spread through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Prevention: Vaccination is the most effective prevention method.

Treatment: Supportive care like rest, fluids, and vitamin A supplementation.

HIV/AIDS

Mode of Transmission: Spread through unprotected sex, sharing needles, and from mother to child during childbirth or breastfeeding.

Prevention: Use of condoms, antiretroviral treatment, and regular testing.

Treatment: Antiretroviral therapy (ART) to manage the virus and improve life expectancy.

Tuberculosis (TB)

Mode of Transmission: Spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Prevention: Vaccination with Bacillus Calmette-Guerin (BCG), good ventilation, and avoiding crowded areas.

Treatment: A combination of antibiotics taken over a 6-9 month period.

Guinea Worm Disease (Dracunculiasis)

Mode of Transmission: Spread through drinking water contaminated with water fleas that carry guinea worm larvae.

Prevention: Drink filtered water, avoid stagnant water, and educate communities on safe water practices.

Treatment: There is no specific treatment; the worm must be slowly extracted from the body over several weeks.

Leishmaniasis

Mode of Transmission: Spread by the bite of infected sandflies.

Prevention: Use insect repellent, sleep under treated bed nets, and wear long clothing in affected areas.

Treatment: Antiparasitic medications such as Amphotericin B and Miltefosine.

Yellow Fever

Mode of Transmission: Spread through the bite of an infected Aedes mosquito.

Prevention: Vaccination is the best prevention, along with mosquito control measures.

Treatment: No specific treatment; supportive care for symptoms like fever and dehydration.

Meningitis

Mode of Transmission: Spread through respiratory droplets, close contact, or contaminated food.

Prevention: Vaccination and avoiding close contact with infected individuals.

Treatment: Antibiotics for bacterial meningitis; antiviral drugs for viral forms, along with supportive care.

Ebola Virus Disease (EVD)

Mode of Transmission: Spread through contact with bodily fluids of infected individuals or contaminated objects.

Prevention: Avoid contact with infected individuals, use personal protective equipment (PPE), and maintain proper hygiene.

Treatment: Supportive care and experimental antiviral treatments; early detection is critical.

Dengue Fever

Mode of Transmission: Spread through the bite of an infected Aedes mosquito.

Prevention: Use insect repellent, wear protective clothing, and reduce mosquito habitats (stagnant water).

Treatment: Supportive care with fluids, pain relievers like acetaminophen, and hospitalization for severe cases.

Ulcer

Mode of Transmission: Not contagious; ulcers form due to factors like Helicobacter pylori infection, long-term use of NSAIDs, and stress.

Prevention: Avoid excessive use of NSAIDs, manage stress, avoid smoking and alcohol, and treat Helicobacter pylori infection.

Treatment: Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs), antibiotics for H. pylori, and lifestyle changes like diet modification.

Hypertension

Mode of Transmission: Not contagious; caused by factors like genetics, obesity, poor diet, and lack of exercise.

Prevention: Maintain a healthy diet low in sodium, exercise regularly, avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.

Treatment: Lifestyle changes, antihypertensive medications like ACE inhibitors, and regular monitoring of blood pressure.

Diabetes

Mode of Transmission: Not contagious; caused by the bodyâ€™s inability to regulate blood sugar, influenced by genetics, diet, and lifestyle.

Prevention: Maintain a healthy diet, exercise regularly, manage weight, and monitor blood sugar levels.

Treatment: Insulin therapy, oral medications, lifestyle changes including diet and exercise.

Heart Disease

Mode of Transmission: Not contagious; caused by factors such as high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, obesity, and genetics.

Prevention: Maintain a healthy diet, exercise regularly, avoid smoking, manage stress, and control risk factors like high blood pressure and diabetes.

Treatment: Lifestyle changes, medications like statins and beta-blockers, and in severe cases, surgical procedures such as angioplasty or bypass surgery.

Giardiasis (Giardia lamblia)

Mode of Transmission: Ingesting water or food contaminated with Giardia cysts.

Prevention: Boiling or filtering drinking water, good sanitation practices.

Treatment: Antiparasitic medications, such as metronidazole.

Hookworm Infection (Ancylostoma duodenale and Necator americanus)

Mode of Transmission: Larvae in contaminated soil penetrate the skin.

Prevention: Wearing shoes, improved sanitation.

Treatment: Antiparasitic medications, such as albendazole and mebendazole.

African Trypanosomiasis (Sleeping Sickness - Trypanosoma brucei)

Mode of Transmission: Spread by the bite of infected tsetse flies.

Prevention: Insect repellents, avoiding infested areas.

Treatment: Antiparasitic drugs, like pentamidine or eflornithine.

Brucellosis (Brucella spp. bacteria)

Mode of Transmission: Consumption of unpasteurized dairy products or contact with infected animals.

Prevention: Avoiding raw dairy, proper handling of animals.

Treatment: Antibiotics like doxycycline and rifampin.

Hepatitis E (Hepatitis E virus)

Mode of Transmission: Consuming contaminated water.

Prevention: Drinking safe water, good sanitation.

Treatment: Typically supportive care; resolves on its own, but ribavirin may be used in severe cases.

Amoebiasis (Entamoeba histolytica)

Mode of Transmission: Fecal-oral route, typically through contaminated food or water.

Prevention: Improved sanitation, handwashing, and clean drinking water.

Treatment: Metronidazole or tinidazole for killing amoebic cysts and parasites.

Streptococcus pneumoniae (Pneumonia)

Mode of Transmission: Airborne droplets from coughs and sneezes.

Prevention: Vaccination, good hygiene, and avoiding close contact with infected people.

Treatment: Antibiotics such as amoxicillin or azithromycin, and supportive care.

Sickle Cell Disease

Mode of Transmission: Genetic disorder inherited from both parents with sickle cell traits.

Prevention: Genetic counseling and testing, especially in populations at higher risk.

Treatment: Pain management, blood transfusions, and medications like hydroxyurea to reduce complications.

Hemophilia

Mode of Transmission: Genetic disorder typically inherited from a parent with the hemophilia gene.

Prevention: Genetic testing and counseling; however, there is no way to prevent the disease if inherited.

Treatment: Regular injections of clotting factor VIII or IX, depending on the type of hemophilia, to prevent excessive bleeding.

Thalassemia

Mode of Transmission: Genetic disorder inherited from one or both parents with mutated hemoglobin genes.

Prevention: Genetic counseling and screening, especially for those with a family history of thalassemia.

Treatment: Regular blood transfusions, iron chelation therapy to remove excess iron, and, in some cases, bone marrow transplant.
